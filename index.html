Maya Angelou

Legendary Civil Rights Activist, Author and Poet (1928–2014)

"A hero is someone who you look up to because of what they have done or because of what they do. Maya Angelou is a heroine because she encourages people to “take life by the lapels”, to face your trials and hardships. She is a person who looks for the positive and never gives up. Her ability to stay strong throughout the early years of her life makes her a hero to me. She sacrificed herself for others so they could have a better life. She shows leadership by writing all these books about her life and telling people to be positive."-Leilani from Kahului

"Angelou studied and began writing poetry at a young age, and used poetry and other great literature to cope with trauma, as she described in her first and most well-known autobiography, I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings. She became a poet after a series of occupations as a young adult, including as a cast member of a European tour of Porgy and Bess, and a performer of calypso music in nightclubs in the 1950s."

She is a woman who has overcome hardships almost all her life. She had the courage and determination to stay strong. Her words stir people up and create insight to a whole new world for her readers which makes her one of the most influential heroine's of the 21st century. The tranformation from all the traumas she endured to her greatest achivements are a great inspiration.

Maya Angelou died at 86.

Background
  • Maya Angelou was born April 4, 1928, in St. Louis, Mo.
  • Her actual birth name is Marguerite Johnson.
  • But when she was 14 years old she dropped out of school to become San Francisco's first African-American female cable car conductor.
  • She gave birth to her first and only child — her son, Guy — when she was 16 years old.
Achievements
  • She never went to college but has received more than 50 honorary degrees.
  • She was presented the Presidential Medal of Freedom by Barack Obama in 2011
  • She helped Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. in the Civil Rights movement as the Northern Coordinator for the Southern Christian Leadership Conference.
  • She was the first black woman director and producer for 20th Century Fox
  • She mastered six other languages besides English, including French, Spanish, Italian, Arabic, and the West African language Fanti.
  • She's the second poet in history to read a poem during the presidential inauguration
  • She won a Grammy for an audio recording of that poem, "On The Pulse of Morning," in the Best Spoken Word category.
  • Over the course of her life, Angelou published seven autobiographies, three books of essays, and several books of poetry.
